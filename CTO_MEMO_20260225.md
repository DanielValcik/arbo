## 3. Critical Issues

### Issue 1: Single-Layer Operation

The system is designed as a 9-layer confluence engine. In practice, only L2 (Value Signal) is generating trade signals (37,335 signals in 24h). All other layers show 0 signals except L8 (Attention) with 25 signals and zero trades.

Confluence scoring is meaningless when only one layer produces signals. Confluence=1 is the maximum possible value, making the confluence gate a no-op. The entire risk architecture depends on multiple independent signals confirming a trade. Without this, we are running single-signal trades — exactly what the system was designed to prevent.

### Issue 2: P&L Is Misleading

The +158% ROI is driven almost entirely by long-dated crypto price prediction markets. Positions entered at $0.15–$0.22 are now marked at $0.83–$0.96, reflecting general BTC/ETH price appreciation — not model edge. Example: "Will Trump acquire Greenland" bought at $0.11, now marked at $0.89 (+541%). This is a directional bet that happened to move favorably, not evidence of systematic edge.

Soccer (our most mechanical category with Pinnacle odds comparison) is -$18.20 on 11 trades with 0 settlements. Too early to evaluate, but this is the category with the most rigorous edge calculation.

### Issue 3: Position Sizing Anomaly

Total invested across 32 open positions is $1,771.75, exceeding the portfolio balance of $1,452.19. Paper trading doesn't enforce fill constraints, but this pattern would cause failures in live trading. The geometric sizing model needs validation against actual available balance and the 5% per-position cap.
